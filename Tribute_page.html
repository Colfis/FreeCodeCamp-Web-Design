Be the change that you wish to see in the world.

A photograph of Mahatma Gandhi. He is wearing his traditional white dhoti and shawl, which symbolize his simplicity and dedication to nonviolence.

Brief Biography

Mahatma Gandhi was an Indian leader known for his philosophy of nonviolence and civil disobedience. He played a crucial role in India's struggle for independence from British rule, inspiring movements for civil rights and freedom worldwide."

Timeline of Key Events

  • 1869 – Born in Porbandar, India.
  • 1888 – Traveled to London to study law.
  • 1893 – Faced racial discrimination inspiring his activism.
  • 1915 - Returned to India and joined the independence movement.
  • 1930 – Led the famous Salt March against British taxes.
  • 1947 – India gained independence.
  • 1948 – Assassinated in New Delhi.

Achievements & Legacy

  • Led India’s independence movement using nonviolent resistance.
  • Inspired global civil rights leaders like Martin Luther King Jr. and Nelson Mandela.
  • Championed social reforms, including caste equality and rural development.

Fun Facts

  • Gandhi walked 18 miles per day for nearly 40 years.
  • He was nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize five times but never won.
  • His birthday, October 2, is celebrated as the International Day of Nonviolence.

Closing Statement

"If humanity is to progress, Gandhi is inescapable. He lived, thought, and acted, inspired by the vision of humanity evolving toward a world of peace and harmony." – Martin Luther King Jr."
